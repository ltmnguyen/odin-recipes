Soba Noodles

Description

On a hot day in Japan, a bowl of cold soba noodles is the perfect meal or snack. Soba noodles are brown noodles made of wheat and buckwheat. They can be stirfried or flash boiled and served cold. Cold soba noodles are usually served with a dipping sauce. The dipping sauce is a dashi-based sauce, which is a common base in Japanese cuisine. These noodles can be cooked and served with any topping, but they are also perfect with just the dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • Salt
  • 1 cup dashi stock
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 8 ounces of soba noodles
  • Minced ginger
  • Scallions, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. In a large pot, boil water and salt it
  2. Cook the soba noodles until tender. They should not be mushy.
  3. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold running water.
  4. To make the dipping sauce, combine dashi stock, soy sauce, and mirin in a small bowl.
  5. Adjust the soy sauce to your liking, and add the scallions.
  6. Serve the noodles with whatever garnishes you prefer with the sauce on the side.
