Kimchi Jjigae

Description

Kimchi Jjigae is a traditional Korean dish, also called Kimchi Stew. It is made with spicy, fermented cabbage, kimchi, and an assortment of other ingredients. This dish can be made with seafood, pork, beef, or tofu as protein. Kimchi jjigae is eaten in both restaurants and households. It is a comforting but spicy dish. You can eat kimchi jjigae with a warm bowl of rice and some traditional korean side dishes. It is easy to make and especially delicious on a cold day!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of kimchi, bite sized
  • 1/2 pound of your protein of choice
  • 1/4 cup of kimchi brine
  • 1 pack of soft tofu
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons gochugaru, Korean chili flakes
  • 1 teaspoon gochujang, Korean chili paste
  • 2 cups anchovuy broth
  • 3 green onions, sliced

Steps

  1. Add the kimchi, protein, and gochugaru to a clay pot and let it cook down.
  2. Add the gochujang, sugar, and salt.
  3. Add the kimchi brine and anchovy stock.
  4. Cook for 10 minutes over medium heat and covered.
  5. Take off the lid and mix through. Lay sliced soft tofu over the top.
  6. Cover and cook for another 10-15 minutes over medium heat.
  7. Garnish with green onions and serve with a warm bowl of rice.
